Jellied Moose Nose

Description:

Jellied Moose Nose is a Canadian abomination delicacy made of cut up moose nose and gelatinized broth.
Dating as far back to a cookbook made in 1967, Jellied Moose Nose has been a staple of Canadian cuisine ever since.

It has been described by Chef Andrew Zimmern as a dish exploding "with little tastes".

Ingredients:

  • Moose Nose
  • Broth
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • other spices (optional)

Cooking Instructions

  1. Cut the upper jaw bone of moose until right below the eyes
  2. Boil moose nose in large kettle of boiling water for 45 minutes
  3. Remove nose from boiling water and chill it in cold water.
  4. Pull out hair from nose (the water should've loosened it).
  5. Fill kettle with freshwater and place nose in.
  6. Now add onion, garlic, vinegar, and whatever other spices you want to the kettle.
  7. Bring to a boil, then cook it on a reduced flame until it's tender.
  8. Let it cool overnight in liquid.
  9. When cool, take moose out of broth and remove bones/cartilage
  10. You should have 2 different types of meat now. White meat from the bulb of nose and dark meat from other places.
  11. Now slice meat into thinly pieces into loaf pan.
  12. Reheat broth to boiming and then pour broth over meat in the loaf pan.
  13. Let it cool until the jelly sets in.
  14. Slice and serve cold
